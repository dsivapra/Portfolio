Predicting Uber Fare
Prices For Future Bookings

Objectives

  • Analyze Uber fare patterns to improve pricing transparency and user satisfaction.
  • Identify the key factors that influence Uber fare prices.
  • Examine how fares vary by time of day, day of the week, and location.
  • Determine the optimal times for users to book rides at the lowest possible rates.
  • Provide actionable insights into Uber's pricing strategy to support better travel planning.
  • Recommend strategies to enhance customer engagement based on fare trend analysis.

    • Methodology

  • Performed exploratory data analysis (EDA) to identify trends in fare prices based on time, distance, and passenger count
  • Cleaned the dataset by removing missing values, invalid coordinates, outliers, and zero-distance trips
  • Created new features from the pickup time (hour, day of the week, month) and calculated trip distance using the Haversine formula
  • Built predictive models including Linear Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest, and XGBoost, with Grid Search for hyperparameter tuning
  • Utilized Topic Modeling (LDA) to extract key themes such as fit, material, and design
  • Used an 80:20 train-test split and 10-fold cross-validation to evaluate performance
  • Selected Random Forest as the final model due to its superior RÂ² and lowest Mean Absolute Error (MAE)

    • Data Overview

    Data Source
    • Kaggle: Uber Fares Dataset

    This dataset contains 200,000 records spanning trips from 2009 to 2015, providing a comprehensive view
    of Uber's fare patterns over time.

    Data Dictionary

    Variable Description
    key A unique identifier for each trip.
    fare_amount The cost of each trip in USD.
    pickup_datetime Date and time when the trip began (meter engaged).
    passenger_count The number of passengers in the vehicle (input by the driver).
    pickup_longitude The longitude where the trip started.
    pickup_latitude The latitude where the trip started.
    dropoff_longitude The longitude where the trip ended (meter disengaged).
    dropoff_latitude The latitude where the trip ended.

    Data Cleaning

    Five steps of data cleaning were performed, reducing the total no. of rows from 200,000 to 179,501.


    Removing Geographic Outliers

    Detecting and removing outliers in Uber pickup data based on latitude and longitude ensures more accurate analysis and reliable predictions.

    Data Manipulation

    Pickup time was processed into month, hour, and weekday, and distances were calculated using the Haversine formula.
